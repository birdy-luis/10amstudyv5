## THE BIG PICTURE

### What is Edge Score?

**Edge Score** is a single number (0-100) that tells you how HIGH QUALITY a trade setup is BEFORE you enter.

It's like a **confidence rating** for a potential trade opportunity.

- **0-40:** Terrible setup, don't trade
- **40-60:** Weak setup, mostly skip
- **60-75:** Okay setup, trade only with extra signals
- **75-85:** Good setup, enter with confidence
- **85-100:** Excellent setup, enter aggressively

### Why Do We Need It?

Without Edge Score, traders do this:
- "Umm, it looks good"
- "I have a feeling about this one"
- "My gut says yes"

This is **guessing**. You lose money guessing.

With Edge Score, traders do this:
- "Score = 78. Check the formula."
- "78 ≥ 75, so I ENTER."
- "Reason: High speed + good probe + solid consistency + normal efficiency"

This is **calculating**. You make money calculating.

### The Edge Score Formula

```
Edge Score = (Speed × 35%) + (Probe × 25%) + (Consistency × 30%) + (Efficiency × 10%)
```

**English Translation:**
- **Speed** (most important): How fast does the reversal start? (35%)
- **Probe** (solid foundation): What type of setup is it? (25%)
- **Consistency** (reliability): Does this pattern usually work? (30%)
- **Efficiency** (profit speed): How many points per minute? (10%)

**Total = 100%** (always)

## COMPONENT 1: SPEED SCORE

### Definition

**Speed Score measures how FAST the market moves to the extreme.**

After the market opens (say, 10:00 AM), it starts moving. At some point, it reaches an extreme (highest high or lowest low) before reversing.

**Speed Score** is: How quickly did it reach that extreme?

### Three Categories

#### FAST (Score = 92/100)

**Definition:** The extreme is reached in **≤2 minutes** from open.

**What it looks like:**
- 10:00 AM: Market opens
- 10:01 AM or 10:02 AM: Extreme reached
- Market has moved decisively and QUICKLY to the turning point

**Why it matters:** 
When the market moves FAST to an extreme, it signals strong directional commitment. The reversal is likely to be LARGE because all that momentum needs to turn around.

## COMPONENT 2: PROBE QUALITY SCORE

### Definition

**Probe Quality Score measures the AMPLITUDE of the initial move before reversal.**

Think of the probe as a test move. The market is "testing" in one direction before it reverses.

**Probe Quality** is: What's the SIZE of that test move?

Bigger test = Higher quality probe = More likely the reversal will be large.

### Four Categories

#### ELITE (Score = 95/100)

**Definition:** The probe amplitude is **50+ points** (large initial move).

**What it looks like:**
- Market opens at 21400
- Market probes DOWN to 21350 (50 point move into support/low)
- Then reverses UP from there
- OR market probes UP to 21450 and reverses DOWN

**Why it matters:**
When the initial probe is LARGE (50+ points), it signals:
1. The market is testing a significant level
2. There's real conviction in the initial direction
3. When it reverses from such a distance, the reversal has "room to run"
4. The traders who went the wrong way have more to lose = more forced buying/selling on reversal

## COMPONENT 3: CONSISTENCY SCORE

### Definition

**Consistency Score measures HOW RELIABLE this pattern/probe type is.**

Consistency is asking: "Based on HISTORICAL DATA, does this setup usually work?"

It's about **pattern reliability**, not about the current setup.

### Three Categories

#### HIGH Consistency (Score = 90/100)

**Definition:** This setup pattern works **85%+ of the time historically**.

**What it means:**
When you see this pattern in the past, it reversed and hit targets 85+ times out of 100.

**Example:**
- You see a FAST speed + ELITE probe setup
- History shows: 92 out of 100 times this combination worked
- So: HIGH consistency (90 score)

**Why it matters:**
HIGH consistency means the pattern is PROVEN. You have data backing you up. The market SHOULD do what it historically did.

---

#### MEDIUM Consistency (Score = 78/100)

**Definition:** This setup pattern works **60-85%** of the time historically.

**What it means:**
Sometimes works, sometimes doesn't. Solid but not bulletproof.

**Example:**
- You see a NORMAL speed + GOOD probe setup
- History shows: 75 out of 100 times this worked
- So: MEDIUM consistency (78 score)

**Why it matters:**
MEDIUM consistency means it's worth trading, but you should consider adding extra confirmation signals before entering.

---

#### LOW Consistency (Score = 60/100)

**Definition:** This setup pattern works **<60%** of the time historically.

**What it means:**
It works sometimes, but it's unreliable. Coin-flip territory.

**Example:**
- You see a LATE speed + STANDARD probe setup
- History shows: 55 out of 100 times this worked
- So: LOW consistency (60 score)

**Why it matters:**
LOW consistency means be CAUTIOUS. Only trade if you have:
1. Extra confirmation signals
2. Perfect risk/reward setup
3. Really high conviction from other factors

## THE FORMULA

Now that you understand each component, here's how they work together:

```
Edge Score = (SPEED_SCORE × 0.35) + (PROBE_SCORE × 0.25) + (CONSISTENCY_SCORE × 0.30) + (EFFICIENCY_SCORE × 0.10)
```

### Why These Weights?

| Component | Weight | Why? |
|-----------|--------|------|
| **SPEED** | 35% | Most predictive of reversal size. Fast moves = large reversals. |
| **CONSISTENCY** | 30% | Reliability matters. Historical proof beats theory. |
| **PROBE** | 25% | Solid foundation. Quality of setup determines confidence. |
| **EFFICIENCY** | 10% | Good indicator but less important than the others. |
| **TOTAL** | 100% | Always balances to 100%. |

### Calculation Steps

**Step 1:** Identify each component score (0-100)
- Speed: 65, 80, or 92
- Probe: 45, 62, 82, or 95
- Consistency: 60, 78, or 90
- Efficiency: 55, 75, or 88

**Step 2:** Multiply each by its weight
- Speed × 0.35
- Probe × 0.25
- Consistency × 0.30
- Efficiency × 0.10

**Step 3:** Add them up
- Total = Result (0-100)

**Step 4:** Interpret
- < 60: SKIP
- 60-75: CONDITIONAL
- ≥ 75: ENTER

## DECISION RULES

### The Decision Framework

After calculating your Edge Score, follow these rules:

#### Rule 1: Edge < 60 = SKIP

**What it means:** Don't trade.

**Why:**
- Weak setup
- Low historical win rate
- Poor risk/reward
- Capital better used elsewhere

**Action:** Go to next trade. Wait.

---

#### Rule 2: Edge 60-75 = CONDITIONAL

**What it means:** You CAN trade, but ONLY if you have extra confirmation.

**Extra Confirmation Signals:**
1. Perfect price level (support/resistance)
2. Volume spike
3. Volatility expansion
4. Previous resistance/support bounce
5. Additional timeframe alignment
6. Multiple pattern setup

**You need AT LEAST 2 extra signals** before entering in the 60-75 zone.

**Action:** Scout for confirmation signals. If found, enter SMALLER size (0.5x).

---

#### Rule 3: Edge ≥ 75 = ENTER

**What it means:** Enter the trade immediately.

**Why:**
- Strong setup
- High historical win rate (85%+)
- Positive risk/reward
- This is your alpha signal

**Sizing by Edge Score:**
- 75-80: Normal size (1.0x)
- 80-85: Medium size (1.25x)
- 85-90: Large size (1.5x)
- 90+: Maximum size (2.0x)

**Additional Rules:**

**If April (high volatility month):**
- Multiply position size by 1.5x
- Example: 85-90 edge with April → 1.5x × 1.5x = 2.25x position

**If High Efficiency (>5 pts/min):**
- Can maintain larger position longer
- Profits come in fast, reduces time at risk

**If ELITE Probe:**
- Can trust the setup more
- Tighter stops possible

**Action:** Enter trade. Follow execution plan. Trail stops. Manage to 70% target.

## COMMON MISTAKES

### Mistake 1: Miscalculating Speed

**The Error:**
You think a 3-minute extreme is FAST (92 score), when it's actually NORMAL (80 score).

**What Happens:**
```
Your calculation:    Edge = 85 (using FAST 92)
Actual should be:    Edge = 78 (using NORMAL 80)
Difference:          7 points overestimate
Consequence:         You enter a 78 thinking it's 85. Marginal trade.
```

**How to Prevent:**
- Use a timer religiously
- 10:00 to 10:02 = FAST
- 10:00 to 10:04 = NORMAL  
- 10:00 to 10:07+ = LATE
- Write it down. Don't estimate.

### Mistake 4: Wrong Weight Calculations

**The Error:**
You use 40% for Speed instead of 35%. Or 15% for Efficiency instead of 10%.

**What Happens:**
```
Correct: Edge = (92 × 0.35) + (82 × 0.25) + (85 × 0.30) + (75 × 0.10) = 83.5
Wrong:   Edge = (92 × 0.40) + (82 × 0.25) + (85 × 0.30) + (75 × 0.05) = 84.25
Drift:   Over time, edges creep higher and higher
Result:  You enter weaker trades thinking they're strong
```

**How to Prevent:**
- Write weights on a post-it: **35-30-25-10**
- Keep it visible on your desk
- Check every calculation against it
- Or use a calculator app with preset formulas

---

### Mistake 5: Ignoring When Market Invalidates Probe

**The Error:**
You enter on a GOOD probe (82 score). Then market gaps past your probe level instantly.

**What Happens:**
```
Before trade: Good probe = high quality setup (correct)
After market moves: Probe is gapped, no longer valid (market changed)
Your entry thesis: Partially invalidated by market action
Your stop: Needs to widen because probe isn't the anchor anymore
Result: Loss or wider loss than expected
```

**How to Prevent:**
- This is NOT a scoring error—the score was correct PRE-trade
- This is market risk—the market invalidated your setup POST-trade
- That's why you have stops. Stops protect you when market breaks your thesis.
- Edge score = entry quality. Stops = risk management. Different things.

---

### Mistake 6: Confusing Probe with Reversal

**The Error:**
You see a 20-point probe and think that's the ENTIRE reversal.

**What Happens:**
```
Probe (initial move): 20 points
Reversal (full move): 150 points (7.5x larger!)
Expectation: Profit 20 points
Reality: Can profit 150 points
Opportunity: Lost by stopping too early
```

**How to Prevent:**
- Probe and reversal are DIFFERENT things
- Probe = initial test move (small)
- Reversal = full counter-move (large)
- Expected reversal = look at historical data for your probe type
- ELITE probes → 240+ pt average reversals
- GOOD probes → 125-200 pt average reversals

---

### Mistake 7: Trading When You're Not Fully Confident

**The Error:**
Edge = 77. You calculate it correctly. But your gut says "something feels off."

**What Happens:**
```
System says: ENTER (edge 77 ≥ 75)
Your gut says: Maybe skip this one?
You enter hesitantly
You exit too early because you lack conviction
You miss the full reversal
Result: Reduced profit or unnecessary loss
```

**How to Prevent:**
- Trust the system. Edge score is objective data.
- If edge ≥75, ENTER. No hesitation.
- If your gut disagrees, the system is probably RIGHT (you're tired, emotional, etc.)
- After the trade ends, review to see if system or gut was correct
- (System wins ~90% of these reviews)
